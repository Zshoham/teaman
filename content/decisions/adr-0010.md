---

## Context

Slow tasks — exports, notifications, re-encryption — are running inline on request threads, causing timeouts and head-of-line blocking on the API.

## Decision

Background work is enqueued to a managed queue and processed by dedicated workers, with retries and a dead-letter queue for poison messages.

## Consequences

- API latency decouples from slow downstream work.
- Workloads absorb spikes by queueing instead of failing.
- Every consumer must now be idempotent to tolerate at-least-once delivery.
