## Context

Early prototypes used a mix of a document store and a relational database. The data is overwhelmingly relational, and operating two stores doubles the backup, migration, and on-call surface for little benefit.

## Decision

PostgreSQL is the default home for transactional data. Specialised stores must be justified per case in their own ADR; the bar is a concrete workload Postgres cannot serve.

## Consequences

- One well-understood operational story for backups, failover, and migrations.
- JSONB covers the genuinely schemaless corners without a second engine.
- Heavy full-text or vector search may eventually warrant a dedicated store.
